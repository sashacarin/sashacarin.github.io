The "s" in sci-fi stands for sexist.

I dont know what the rest of the letters stand for, but thats probably what the s stands for. I guess I've been thinking a lot about this lately, not because I deeply felt the effects of sexism in science fiction, but because I wonder what its effects have been.

I think a common theme in so much sci-fi is trying to grasp what "alien science" might be. Even just recently, Hail Mary by Andy Wier had its big moment with the Gosling movie coming out, and everyone thinking NASA is cool again. The whole reason that story is so fun is because a human is able to use principles of math and science to communicate with an alien! In practice though, even though physics is invariant (hopefully) theres absolutely nothing to say that a different form of intellegence would comprehend those physiscal properties in the way that we do. Imagine an intellegence that was specificaly good at comprehending things in 4 dimensions, where lots math and topology is completely wacky to us. Or what would the scientific method mean to something thinking in parrallel processes?

Theres more here, something for another post though because the real question is; Does it matter that there's no chicks in Sci-Fi? The answer is obviously yes, if only for the matter of the inspiration of future generations of scientists. But aside from the general injustices and waste of perfectly good brains, the question persists, Is the Female experience so "alien" to the Male as to cause a divergence in our current conception of science?

I will tenatively answer yes ... Not for a difference in genetics, or phsyical(mental) ability, but because of culture.

I think you might now see that this is a pretty thinly veiled seguey to saying that; "Neil Stephenson is the Best Author in History". Note that this is a hill I am willing to die on. Ill start here.
